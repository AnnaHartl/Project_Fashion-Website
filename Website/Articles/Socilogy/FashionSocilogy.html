Socilogy

by Tamara Melcher


What does it mean?


The possibility to wear what we want is for the people a kind of freedom, but that way you automatically get associated to group. There so many groups all around the world. Too much to count all of them right here.

By these associations are women more affected than men. If a woman wears a short skirt, everybody says she is cheap and a slut. But who cares if a man wears no shirt?

Sometimes we also want to adapt ourselves to our generation and the style of it, to delimit us from the older ones. I also have to say that most of these associations happen by teenagers. But as always money takes a very important part in this associations. Because the ones who have more money than others will delimit themselves, because they think they are better because they wear branded clothes.


Influence


Most people buy something that is trendy, even when they don't like it. They let themselves get influenced by others, they just want to belong to a group. Of course, social media and ads have an important role. For example if Kylie Jenner says that maxi skirts look stupid, most of her followers would never buy one again.


Groups

There are two main groups:
the conservatively shaped group and the experiment-friendly, rebellious and individualistic group.


Individualistic group

The individualistic group applies new fashion trends faster than the other group, because they want to stand out from the mass. This group wants to represent their personality by their clothes. They want to delimit themselves from others to show different generations and different styles. Mostly these people are teenagers, who want to find out their own style without the influence from their parents.

Conservative group

But also, the conservatively shaped group applies new styles with time, but not because they really want a new style, it is because they want to “compete”. As soon as this happens these fashion trneds are not very interesting anymore for the individualistic group.
